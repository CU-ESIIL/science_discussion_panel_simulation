# Societal Impact Checklist for Urban Wildlife Corridors Manuscript

**Purpose:** Ensure the manuscript responsibly addresses policy relevance, equity, and societal implications before submission.

---

## ✅ Checklist

### 1. Policy‑Relevant Language
- [ ] Identify any statements that directly recommend policy changes or regulatory actions.
- [ ] Verify that all policy recommendations are supported by peer‑reviewed evidence or established guidelines.
- [ ] Ensure language is **non‑prescriptive** where uncertainty exists (e.g., use “may inform policy” instead of “should enforce”).
- [ ] Flag any use of legal terminology; confirm alignment with existing statutes or municipal codes.
- [ ] Prepare a **policy‑impact statement** summarizing recommended actions for policymakers.

### 2. Equity & Inclusion Considerations
- [ ] Assess whether the study addresses impacts on vulnerable or marginalized communities (e.g., low‑income neighborhoods, Indigenous lands).
- [ ] Document any data gaps related to socio‑economic or demographic factors.
- [ ] Evaluate potential disparate impacts of recommended wildlife corridors (e.g., displacement, gentrification).
- [ ] Include recommendations for community engagement and participatory planning.
- [ ] Ensure citations respect community‑led research and give proper credit.

### 3. Ethical & Societal Implications
- [ ] Discuss potential unintended consequences (e.g., human‑wildlife conflict, habitat alteration).
- [ ] Address animal welfare considerations of corridor design and management.
- [ ] Consider the cultural significance of urban wildlife to local communities.
- [ ] Highlight any environmental justice implications.

### 4. Required Human Review Steps
- [ ] **PI Liaison Review:** Confirm that all policy statements align with the project’s scope and the PI’s intent.
- [ ] **Skeptic Review:** Verify evidence backing policy recommendations and assess risk of over‑statement.
- [ ] **Societal Impact Agent Review:** Ensure equity and ethical considerations are comprehensively covered.
- [ ] **Legal/Compliance Check (if applicable):** Confirm no infringement on existing regulations or community rights.
- [ ] **Final Sign‑off:** Obtain explicit approval from the PI before manuscript submission.

---

*Note:* Any item left unchecked must be addressed before the manuscript proceeds to the next review stage.
