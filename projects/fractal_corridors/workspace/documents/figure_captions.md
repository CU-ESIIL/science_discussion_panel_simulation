# Figure Captions

**Figure 1: Emergence of Clusters**

A scatter plot illustrating the emergence of two distinct clusters in a two-dimensional space. Points in Cluster A (steelblue) are centered around (2, 2) while points in Cluster B (orange) are centered around (5, 5). The visualization demonstrates how distinct groupings can arise from underlying data distributions.

**Figure 2: Spatial Gradient (Gaussian Hill)**

A contour-filled heatmap representing a smooth spatial gradient modeled as a Gaussian hill. The intensity peaks at the center (0.5, 0.5) and decays radially outward. This figure is useful for visualizing continuous environmental gradients such as temperature or pollutant concentration.

**Figure 3: Constrained vs Unconstrained Linear Fit**

A comparison of an unconstrained ordinary least‑squares regression (blue line) with a constrained fit that forces the slope to be non‑negative (red dashed line). Synthetic data points are shown in gray. The constrained model illustrates how imposing realistic bounds (e.g., non‑negative growth rates) can alter fitted relationships.
