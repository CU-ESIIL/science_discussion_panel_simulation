# Citation Targets - Urban Wildlife Corridors

Status: search and verification needed. This file intentionally does not assert citations yet.

## Topics to curate

- Urban wildlife corridor design and empirical corridor performance.
- Landscape connectivity models, including graph-based and circuit-theory approaches.
- Least-cost paths, resistance surfaces, and limitations of pairwise shortest-path design.
- Metacommunity coexistence under dispersal limitation and environmental heterogeneity.
- Dendritic ecological networks, including river networks and branching habitat systems.
- Biological and engineered transport networks as analogs for hierarchical corridor design.
- Multi-species corridor planning and tradeoffs among taxa with different movement requirements.

## Evidence needed before manuscript promotion

- Empirical evidence that common corridor workflows emphasize shortest paths or pairwise fragment connections.
- Evidence for or against hierarchical corridor structures improving ecological movement.
- Support for selected population dynamics assumptions and plausible parameter ranges.
- Literature on urban planning constraints, equity, access, and governance before any public recommendations.
