# SOCIETAL_IMPACT_CHECKLIST.md - Sensitive Claim and Translation Review

Use this checklist before translating scientific work into public, policy, community, legal, health, or planning language.

## Policy-Relevant Language

- [ ] Identify statements that imply a policy, planning, regulatory, or management recommendation.
- [ ] Verify that recommendations are supported by appropriate evidence and review.
- [ ] Use cautious language where uncertainty remains.
- [ ] Flag legal terminology for human review.
- [ ] Keep exploratory results separate from guidance.

## Equity, Community, and Sovereignty

- [ ] Identify affected communities and known data gaps.
- [ ] Flag claims about Tribes, Indigenous knowledge, sovereignty, or community priorities for human review.
- [ ] Document whether community-led sources or governance constraints are relevant.
- [ ] Avoid implying consent, representation, or endorsement without evidence.
- [ ] Record risks of misuse or overgeneralization.

## Public Health, Safety, and Sensitive Data

- [ ] Flag public health or safety claims for human review.
- [ ] Check whether data are sensitive, private, restricted, or governed by license or agreement.
- [ ] Avoid publishing locations, attributes, or interpretations that create foreseeable harm.

## Required Review

- [ ] PI Liaison has batched unresolved human questions.
- [ ] Citation & Evidence Curator has checked claim support.
- [ ] Skeptic / Adversarial Reviewer has reviewed major claims.
- [ ] Societal Impact / Translation Agent has flagged audience and misuse risks.
- [ ] Human PI has explicitly approved external-facing language.
