# Contribution Guide

This workspace supports collaborative scientific synthesis. Contributions should preserve evidence, provenance, uncertainty, and human review.

## Contribution Norms

- Keep claims tied to sources, analyses, figures, or explicit assumptions.
- Prefer small, reversible edits over large undocumented rewrites.
- Make disagreement visible when evidence is incomplete.
- Record major decisions in `DECISIONS.md`.
- Record assumptions in `ASSUMPTIONS.md`.
- Register important artifacts in `documents/ARTIFACT_REGISTRY.md`.
- Use `QUESTIONS_FOR_USER.md` for questions that need the PI Liaison to ask the human lead.
- Do not treat agent consensus as publication approval.

## Attribution

Use meeting notes, artifact metadata, commit messages, and report acknowledgments to preserve who contributed scientific ideas, data, code, review, and interpretation.

## Review

Before promoting outputs, check:

- Evidence and citations
- Data provenance and permissions
- Reproducibility of scripts and figures
- Skeptic review of major claims
- Societal impact review when relevant
- Human approval for public release
