# ROADMAP.md - Scientific Panel Digital Twin Roadmap

| Phase | Purpose | Lead roles | Expected artifacts | Exit criteria |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Phase 0: Panel setup | Define topic, cadence, dashboard needs, review boundaries, and role owners. | PI Liaison; Scientific Director; Decision Recorder | Intake notes; initial `DECISIONS.md`; action owners | Human lead agrees the scope and review requirements are explicit. |
| Phase 1: Research question and theory of change | Convert broad interest into answerable questions and a theory of how evidence informs action. | Scientific Director; Domain Scientist; Societal Impact Agent | Research question memo; theory-of-change sketch; audience map | Research questions are specific enough to guide evidence and analysis. |
| Phase 2: Data inventory and provenance | Identify candidate datasets, access methods, licenses, limitations, and citation needs. | Data Engineer / Infrastructure Scientist; Citation & Evidence Curator; Domain Scientist | Data inventory; provenance table; license notes | Data sources have documented access paths, constraints, and citation requirements. |
| Phase 3: Structured discussion memory | Establish event fields, tags, owners, decision records, norms, and dashboard exports. | Discussion Intelligence Agent; Decision Recorder; Team Science Facilitator | Structured event records; tag map; norm log | The dashboard can summarize discussion state without scraping raw text alone. |
| Phase 4: Exploratory analysis | Explore patterns, anomalies, missingness, spatial/temporal coverage, and initial relationships. | Quantitative Modeler; Domain Scientist; Data Engineer / Infrastructure Scientist | EDA notes; preliminary tables; exploratory figures | Findings are labeled exploratory and key data issues are documented. |
| Phase 5: Modeling and uncertainty | Run models or quantitative syntheses with diagnostics, uncertainty, and sensitivity checks. | Quantitative Modeler; Domain Scientist; Skeptical Reviewer | Model scripts; diagnostics; uncertainty memo | Model assumptions, diagnostics, and limitations are explicit. |
| Phase 6: Figures and interpretation | Create figures and interpret results with provenance and caveats. | Quantitative Modeler; Domain Scientist; Scientific Narrative Lead | Figure files; figure provenance notes; interpretation memo | Each figure links to a script/data source and has reviewed caveats. |
| Phase 7: Skeptical review | Stress-test claims, assumptions, data provenance, and alternative explanations. | Skeptical Reviewer; Citation and Evidence Curator; Scientific Director | Skeptical review memo; revisions list; unresolved objections | Major objections are resolved, accepted as limitations, or escalated to human review. |
| Phase 8: Writing and citation audit | Draft the scientific narrative and audit every major claim against evidence. | Scientific Narrative Lead; Citation and Evidence Curator; Decision Recorder | Draft report/manuscript; citation audit; claim-evidence map | Claims are traceable, caveated, and approved for human review. |
| Phase 9: Impact translation | Translate reviewed findings into responsible audience-specific language. | Societal Impact Agent; Scientific Director; Domain Scientist | Impact memo; audience notes; misuse/risk notes | Translation stays within reviewed evidence and sensitive claims are flagged. |
| Phase 10: Operations and release package | Assemble reproducible outputs, dashboard exports, runtime notes, and release checklist. | Agent Operations Manager; Cloud Infrastructure Engineer; Scientific Narrative Lead | Release README; dashboard export; final artifacts; review checklist | Human lead approves publication, sharing, or archiving. |
